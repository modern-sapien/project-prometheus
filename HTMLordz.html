Here's the Deal

HTML, is pretty straight forward, I think, but let's totally find out together. This will be some super rough, scatter shots of what we're working with when we talk about HTML.

HTML?
HYPER TEXT MARKUP LANGUAGE
If the word HYPER doesn't tell you it's a party, I don't know what will.

<!DOCTYPE html>
You always declare this at the top of your page to let the internet know you mean business. If you haven't already, download HTML Boilplate in Visual Studio Code, make sure it is enabled.
When you're starting a new HTML doc.. just write HTML5, and scroll down to boiler plate & you're done.

<head>
This is where you delcare things and link CSS & declare things like your title.
remember that your CSS will read from first to last, so if you're doing custom CSS in addition to bootstrap, put your CSS link underneath.

<Title>
You gotta open and close this element, within it you declare what your page is about, This is what users see in tabs on top of their window.

<body>
The body is where your content exists. You don't put cute photos of kitties in the HEAD section, or between the closing of body & html. You put those kitties in the body.

SEMANTIC HTML
Here is an unordered list of semantic elements in HTML, all semantic HTML means is that you exist in a world of divs, and sometimes having things called something other than < div = "container sm-12 mt-2 mr-2 pd-1 bg-dark> will be helpful for you as a person and make your webpages more easy to navigate for folks using screenreaders & improve SEO.

How do you like your lists? Ordered? or Unordered? These are your two options, and you already saw one of them earlier. You summon these demons into your HTML by using <ul> for starting an unordered list, <ol> for ordered list. You need to close these tags.

Styling
You can style things in HTML, but you shouldn't so we're not even going to talk about that. Don't do it. Any styling you need to do, create a class or an ID for it and access it in your CSS document.
